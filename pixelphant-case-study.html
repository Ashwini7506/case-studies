Pixelphant Delivery Efficiency — PM Case Study

01 — Framework

What actually drives on-time delivery?

Lever A (Workflow Clarity) × Lever B (Team Coordination) = Delivery Performance
Lever A — Focus
Increase workflow clarity and process standardization

A project that moves through unclear handoffs or inconsistent quality checks will always miss deadlines. Standardizing workflows reduces rework and accelerates delivery.

Lever B — Focus
Improve team coordination across design, QA, and upload stages

When designers, QA, and upload teams work in silos, communication gaps cause delays. Real-time visibility and clear handoffs eliminate bottlenecks.

02 — The Problem

Where delivery timelines are actually breaking

30-40% Projects that miss initial delivery timelines due to unclear requirements or handoff delays
2-3 days Average delay caused by rework when quality issues are caught late in the process
15-20% SKUs that require rework due to inconsistent quality standards across designers
50-5000 SKU volume range that makes batch prioritization and resource allocation critical

The problem isn't that Pixelphant lacks skilled designers. It's that unclear client briefs, manual handoffs between teams, and inconsistent quality checks hit before the project can ship on time. After that, client trust erodes and rework piles up.

Problem Breakdown

Delivery collapses at team handoffs

  • Lever A broken: Workflow clarity → 0. Designers don't know what "good" looks like. QA standards are subjective. Rework is inevitable.
  • Lever B broken: Team coordination → manual. No real-time visibility. Handoffs are email-based. Information gets lost.

Root causes of delivery delays

  • Client briefs are ambiguous or incomplete — leads to misinterpretation and rework
  • No single source of truth for project status — teams operate in silos
  • Quality benchmarks vary by designer — inconsistent output quality
  • Manual handoffs between design → QA → upload — delays at every stage
  • High-volume batches (1000+ SKUs) overwhelm capacity without clear prioritization

04 — Solution

What product should build

The gap isn't talent or tools. It's workflow visibility and process standardization. Teams need a single source of truth for project status, clear quality benchmarks, and automated handoffs.

Solution 1 · Client Brief Standardization + Intake Tool

Before projects start, ensure requirements are crystal clear with a structured intake process.

  1. Build intake form that forces clients to specify: editing style, background type, shadow requirements, color correction needs
  2. Show visual examples for each option so client can click what they want (reduces ambiguity)
  3. Flag incomplete briefs automatically: "You haven't specified background color preference"
  4. Create approval checkpoint: Client reviews sample edit before full batch starts
  5. Store brief as single source of truth accessible to all teams in real-time

Solution 2 · Workflow Dashboard with Real-Time Status

Build internal dashboard that shows every project's status across design → QA → upload stages.

  1. Track each SKU through pipeline: "SKU_123: Design (In Progress) → QA (Waiting) → Upload (Not Started)"
  2. Surface bottlenecks visually: "QA queue: 150 SKUs waiting. Designer capacity available."
  3. Alert teams when handoff is ready: "Designer completed 50 SKUs. QA notified automatically."
  4. Show client-facing timeline: "Batch expected completion: March 30. Current progress: 60%."
  5. Flag at-risk projects: "Project ABC is 2 days behind schedule. Recommend prioritization."

Solution 3 · Quality Checklist + Automated QA Scoring

Standardize quality benchmarks so every designer knows what "good" looks like.

  1. Create quality checklist per editing type: Background removal (clean edges, no artifacts), Shadow (realistic angle, consistent depth)
  2. Build QA scoring system: Each SKU scored on 5 criteria (0-10 scale). Pass threshold: 8+.
  3. Surface common errors per designer: "Designer A frequently scores low on color correction. Recommend training."
  4. Track rework rate per project: "Project XYZ rework rate: 18%. Identify root cause."
  5. Celebrate consistency: "Designer B has 95% first-pass quality. Share best practices."
05 — Success Metrics

How we know it's working

Key Metrics to Track

  • On-Time Delivery Rate: % of projects delivered by committed date (target: 85-90%)
  • Rework Rate per Project: % of SKUs requiring re-editing (target: <10%)
  • Average Handoff Time: Time between design complete → QA start → upload complete (target: <4 hours per stage)
  • First-Pass Quality Score: % of SKUs passing QA on first review (target: 85%+)
  • Client Satisfaction (NPS): Track post-project satisfaction (target: 8+ score)
