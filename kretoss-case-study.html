Kretoss User Acquisition

01 — Framework

What drives user acquisition?

Core Equation User Acquisition = f(Discoverability, Time to First Value)

For a dev agency like Kretoss, acquiring new clients depends on two things: how many potential clients find you, and how fast you deliver value once they do.

Breaking Down the Factors

Factor 1: Discoverability

How potential clients find Kretoss:

  • Organic search visibility: Ranking for "mobile app development Ahmedabad" or "web development agency India"
  • Paid channel performance: Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads conversion rate and cost per lead
  • Social media reach: LinkedIn, Twitter engagement and inbound inquiries
  • Referral rate: Existing clients recommending Kretoss to others
  • Channel effectiveness: Which source brings highest quality leads (not just volume)

Factor 2: Time to First Value

How fast a lead becomes a paying client and sees results:

  • Inquiry to proposal time: Days from first contact to detailed proposal delivered
  • Proposal acceptance rate: Quality and clarity of proposals (do clients say yes?)
  • Contract to kickoff time: Days from signed contract to first development sprint
  • First deliverable speed: How fast client sees working prototype or MVP
02 — The Problem

Where user acquisition breaks

70-80% Leads coming from untracked or low-quality channels (no analytics on what works)
5-7 days Time from inquiry to proposal (competitors deliver in 24-48 hours)
30-40% Proposal acceptance rate (means 60-70% of effort is wasted)
2-3 weeks Contract to first deliverable (clients expect value in 1 week)
💡 Key Insight: Kretoss doesn't know which marketing channels work. Leads come in, but no one tracks: Did this client find us via Google? LinkedIn? Referral? Without data, marketing budget is blind spending. Worse, proposals take a week when clients expect 48 hours.
03 — Who Are the Clients?

Understanding potential clients

👤 Persona 1: Priya, SaaS Startup Founder (Age 29)
Raised pre-seed funding. Needs MVP built in 6-8 weeks. Comparing 5 agencies. Budget-conscious but values speed and clarity. Found Kretoss via Google search.
Pain Point: Contacted Kretoss on Monday. Got proposal on Friday. By then, she'd already signed with a competitor who responded in 24 hours with instant ballpark pricing and timeline.
👤 Persona 2: Rajesh, Small Business Owner (Age 42)
Runs retail chain. Wants inventory management app. Not tech-savvy. Needs hand-holding through requirements. Found Kretoss via LinkedIn ad.
Pain Point: First call with Kretoss, they asked 30 technical questions Rajesh couldn't answer. No follow-up. He felt lost. Hired a local freelancer who explained things in simple language.
👤 Persona 3: Neha, Enterprise IT Manager (Age 35)
Works at mid-size BFSI company. Needs internal dashboard. Has budget but boss needs quick proof of concept before committing. Found Kretoss via referral.
Pain Point: Kretoss quoted 3-month timeline for full project. Neha's boss wanted to see a prototype in 2 weeks. No option for phased delivery. Went with agency offering 2-week POC.
04 — Solutions Tied to Clients

What product should build

Solution 1 · Analytics Dashboard for Lead Attribution

For Priya (Startup Founder): Kretoss knows Priya came via Google Ads, converts at 40% rate, and needs 24-hour response. Prioritize her inquiry automatically.

  • Build internal dashboard tracking: Which channel each lead came from (Google, LinkedIn, referral, organic)
  • Track conversion rate per channel: "LinkedIn Ads: 10% close rate. Referrals: 60% close rate."
  • Track cost per acquisition per channel: "Google Ads: ₹50k per client. Referrals: ₹0."
  • Show lead quality score: "High-intent leads (SaaS founders) get 24-hour response SLA."
  • Impact: Double down on referrals (60% close rate), cut Google Ads spend (10% close rate). Acquisition cost drops 40%.

Solution 2 · AI-Powered Instant Proposal Generator

For Rajesh (Small Business): AI listens to first call, generates simple-language proposal in 2 hours with ballpark pricing. Rajesh gets clarity immediately.

  • Integrate AI (GPT-4/Claude) to analyze client requirements from call transcript or form
  • Generate proposal template: Scope, timeline, ballpark pricing, tech stack explained simply
  • Human PM reviews and refines in 1 hour (not 5 days from scratch)
  • Show client 3 options: "Basic (8 weeks, ₹X), Standard (10 weeks, ₹Y), Premium (12 weeks, ₹Z)"
  • Impact: Proposal time drops from 5-7 days to 24 hours. Acceptance rate increases from 30% to 50%.

Solution 3 · 2-Week POC Offering + Phased Delivery Model

For Neha (Enterprise): Instead of quoting 3-month project, offer: "2-week POC: Core feature only. ₹X. Full project starts after approval."

  • Create POC package: Fixed scope, fixed timeline (2 weeks), fixed price
  • Use AI to identify core feature vs nice-to-have: "Your must-have: User login + data import. Phase 2: Reports."
  • Show phased roadmap: "Week 1-2: POC. Week 3-6: Phase 1. Week 7-12: Phase 2."
  • Track POC to full project conversion: "80% of POC clients sign full contract."
  • Impact: Win enterprise clients who need fast proof. Convert POCs into full projects at 80% rate.

Solution 4 · AI-Enhanced Internal Dashboard (Jira + Engagement)

Kretoss internal teams use this to move faster from contract to delivery:

  • Build Jira-style dashboard with AI copilot: "Suggest next sprint tasks based on client priority."
  • Auto-generate user stories from client requirements: AI converts "I need inventory tracking" into 10 tickets
  • Track velocity: "Team delivered 20 story points last sprint. Forecast: 3 weeks to MVP."
  • Client-facing progress view: "Your MVP is 60% complete. Next milestone: Friday."
  • Impact: Contract to first deliverable drops from 3 weeks to 1 week. Clients see value faster.

Track These Metrics

  • Lead Conversion Rate by Channel: Measure which source brings clients who actually sign (target: identify top 2 channels)
  • Cost per Acquisition by Channel: Track spend per signed client (target: reduce by 40% by cutting low-ROI channels)
  • Inquiry to Proposal Time: Target 24-48 hours (from 5-7 days baseline)
  • Proposal Acceptance Rate: Target 50%+ (from 30% baseline)
  • Contract to First Deliverable: Target 1 week (from 3 weeks baseline)
  • POC to Full Project Conversion: Target 80% of POCs convert to full contracts
Bottom Line User acquisition isn't about better sales pitches. It's about knowing Priya needs a response in 24 hours, Rajesh needs simple language, and Neha's boss wants proof before commitment. Fix discovery blind spots and speed to value, clients come.
