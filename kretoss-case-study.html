Kretoss Client Retention

01 — Framework

What drives client retention?

Core Equation Client Satisfaction Score × Repeat Project Rate = Client Retention

Breaking Down the Metrics

Client Satisfaction Score

Depends on:

  • On-time delivery rate: Projects delivered by committed deadline
  • Feature match accuracy: Built features align with client expectations
  • Communication responsiveness: How fast team responds to client questions/changes
  • Post-launch support quality: Bug fixes and maintenance after delivery

Repeat Project Rate

Depends on:

  • Client engagement post-delivery: Staying in touch after project ends
  • Upsell opportunity identification: Spotting when client needs new features
  • Competitive differentiation: Why clients choose Kretoss over cheaper offshore options
  • Time to second project: How long after first project before client returns
02 — The Problem

Where client retention breaks

30-40% Clients who complete one project and never return for a second
20-30% Projects that miss initial deadline due to scope creep or unclear requirements
2-3 weeks Delay in project start due to misaligned expectations during discovery
60-90 days Gap after project delivery with zero client engagement (ghost period)
💡 Key Insight: Clients don't leave because Kretoss lacks technical skills. They leave because post-delivery, Kretoss ghosts them. No check-ins, no proactive suggestions, no relationship. When the client needs something new 3 months later, they've already moved to a competitor.
03 — Who Are the Clients?

Understanding client needs

👤 Persona 1: Rajesh, Startup Founder (Age 32)
Built an MVP with Kretoss for his EdTech startup. Raised seed funding, now needs to add payment gateway and analytics dashboard. Budget-conscious but values reliability.
Pain Point: After MVP delivery, Kretoss never followed up. Rajesh needed Phase 2 features but didn't know if Kretoss was available. Found a cheaper freelancer on Upwork instead.
👤 Persona 2: Meera, Small Business Owner (Age 45)
Owns a jewelry business. Hired Kretoss to build e-commerce website. Tech-unsavvy, needs ongoing support for adding products, updating prices, fixing small bugs.
Pain Point: After website launch, Meera had questions about adding new products. Kretoss support was slow (2-3 days response time). She hired a local developer for retainer support instead.
👤 Persona 3: Amit, Enterprise Product Manager (Age 38)
Works at mid-size BFSI company. Hired Kretoss for internal dashboard tool. Needs monthly iterations and bug fixes. Has budget but demands professionalism and clear roadmap.
Pain Point: Kretoss delivered the dashboard but never proposed a maintenance plan or roadmap for v2. Amit's manager asked, "What's next?" Amit had no answer. Switched to agency with clear quarterly roadmap proposals.
04 — Solutions Tied to Clients

What product should build

Solution 1 · Post-Delivery Engagement Playbook

For Rajesh (Startup Founder): 30 days post-delivery, PM sends: "Your MVP analytics show 500 users. Ready to add payment gateway? Here's a proposal and timeline."

  • Automated 30-day, 60-day, 90-day check-in emails with data insights from their app
  • Proactive feature suggestions based on user behavior: "We noticed X. Consider adding Y feature."
  • Quick-win proposals: "Add payment in 2 weeks for $X. Here's the scope."
  • Track client engagement: Did they open email? Did they reply? Flag cold clients for manual outreach.
  • Impact: Repeat project rate increases from 60% to 80% within 90 days

Solution 2 · Tiered Support Plans + Proactive Bug Monitoring

For Meera (Small Business Owner): After launch, offer: "Bronze Plan: $200/month for 5 hours support. We monitor your site and fix bugs proactively."

  • Tiered support plans: Bronze (5 hrs/month), Silver (10 hrs/month), Gold (20 hrs/month + priority)
  • Proactive monitoring: Alert client if site is down, slow, or has errors (before client notices)
  • Monthly health report: "Your site served 2,000 users last month. Load time: 1.2s. 3 minor bugs fixed."
  • Easy onboarding: "Try Bronze free for 30 days. Cancel anytime."
  • Impact: 40% of clients subscribe to support plan, creating recurring revenue

Solution 3 · Quarterly Roadmap Proposals

For Amit (Enterprise PM): Before project ends, deliver: "Here's your v1. Here's our proposed v2 roadmap for Q2. Let's discuss priorities."

  • Before project delivery, create 3-month roadmap proposal for next phase
  • Show features as "Must-Have" vs "Nice-to-Have" with effort estimates
  • Include competitive analysis: "Your competitor added X feature. Should we prioritize?"
  • Make it easy to approve: "Option A: Full roadmap ($Y). Option B: Top 3 features only ($Z)."
  • Impact: 60% of enterprise clients approve Phase 2 before Phase 1 even ends
