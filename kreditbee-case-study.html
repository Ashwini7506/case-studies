Case Study: Improving Customer Experience and Retention at Kreditbee

The Problem

Kreditbee operates in a competitive fintech lending landscape where customer expectations for transparency, speed, and support quality are constantly rising. The company faces three interrelated challenges:

  • Transparency Gap: Users complain about unclear loan terms, hidden fees, and confusing application processes. Customers feel misinformed about their obligations and loan conditions.
  • Support Friction: As demand scales, customers struggle to reach support, experience long wait times, and lack clarity on issue resolution timelines.
  • Application Rejection Clarity: Users denied loans don't understand why, leading to frustration and reputational damage.

Business Impact: These problems directly reduce Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV) through increased churn, lower referrals, and negative word-of-mouth in a market where trust is paramount.

Key Performance Indicators

To address this problem, we need to focus on these two metrics:

KPI 1: Loan Application Completion Rate and User Understanding Score

What percentage of users who start the loan application complete it, and how many report understanding the terms clearly? This measures onboarding clarity and friction.

Why it matters: Low completion rates signal confusion or trust issues. Users who don't understand terms are more likely to churn or default.

KPI 2: Customer Support Resolution Time and Satisfaction Score

How quickly can support resolve customer issues? What percentage of customers feel their issues were resolved satisfactorily?

Why it matters: Slow support hurts retention and CLTV. Customers with unresolved issues become advocates for competitors.

Product-Level Solutions

Here's how I would approach improving these KPIs:

Solution 1: Redesign Loan Application Onboarding for Clarity

Users land in Kreditbee's app confused about what they can borrow, what interest rates mean, and what happens after approval. I would redesign the entire application journey with three principles:

  • Expectation setting upfront: Show users exactly what to expect: "This takes 5 minutes. You'll see your loan decision in 2 hours."
  • Progressive disclosure: Instead of one long form, break the application into clear steps. Each step shows what data is being collected and why.
  • Transparent terms visualization: Show loan details (amount, rate, tenure, EMI) as soon as users input their basic info. Let them see the impact of changing values.

This mirrors the onboarding redesign I did at OutX.ai, which improved D1 retention from 5% to 35% by removing confusion upfront.

Expected impact on KPI 1: Increase application completion rate by 20-30% and boost clarity scores significantly.

Solution 2: Build an Application Status and Support Intelligence Tool

Users who are rejected don't know why. Users waiting for approval don't know what's happening. This creates anxiety and support tickets.

I would build a real-time status dashboard that shows:

  • Application progress: "Your application is in step 3 of 5. We're verifying your income." Not just "pending."
  • Rejection clarity: If rejected, show exactly why: "Your credit score is just below our minimum. Here's what you can improve."
  • Proactive support: Before users contact support, surface answers to common questions. "Can't reach me? Here's your loan status..."

This is similar to FunnelMind, the churn detection tool I built at OutX.ai, which surfaced user behavior and reduced support tickets from 80 to 13 per week by giving users the clarity they needed.

Expected impact on KPI 2: Reduce support inquiries by 40%, improve resolution time by 50%, and increase satisfaction scores by 25%.

User Personas Targeted

These solutions would benefit:

Persona 1: First-Time Borrowers (New to Fintech)

Users applying for their first personal loan through an app. They're anxious about rates, confused about terms, and need reassurance at every step. Clear onboarding reduces their friction and builds trust.

Persona 2: Repeat Borrowers with Declining Satisfaction

Users who've borrowed before but face rejections or long wait times. They're at risk of switching to competitors. Transparency on why they're rejected keeps them engaged (even if denied this time).

Persona 3: Active Support Seekers

Users who reach out to support frequently because they're confused or anxious about their applications. Self-service clarity reduces their support burden and improves their experience.

Conclusion

Kreditbee's biggest challenge isn't technology or capital. It's clarity. Users don't trust what they don't understand. When users are confused about terms, rejection reasons, or support timelines, they churn and recommend competitors.

By redesigning the application onboarding and building transparency tools, Kreditbee can:

  • Increase application completion rates by 20-30%
  • Reduce support tickets by 40%
  • Improve Customer Lifetime Value through lower churn and higher referrals
