Kreditbee CLV — PM Case Study

01 — Framework

What actually drives CLV?

Lever A (Retention Duration) × Lever B (Repeat Loan Frequency) = CLV
Lever A — Focus
Increase retention duration

A user who churns after the first loan contributes minimally to CLV. Fixing first-loan experience is the fastest way to extend how long a user stays.

Lever B — Focus
Increase repeat loan frequency

Fintech unit economics only work at 2-3 loans per year per user. A user stuck at 1 total loan makes acquisition spend structurally unprofitable.

02 — The Problem

Where CLV is actually breaking

40-50% Users who never apply for second loan after initial confusion or rejection
3-5 days Critical window after first application before user loses interest or switches
60%+ Application abandonment rate due to lengthy process and unclear requirements
<20% First-time visitor to borrower conversion without effective onboarding

The problem isn't that users dislike Kreditbee. It's that unclear loan terms, confusing eligibility, and rejection without explanation hit before the trust loop can form. After that, switching to Bajaj Finserv or MoneyTap costs nothing.

Root Cause
Loan application process lacks transparency on terms, rates, eligibility
→
User Experience
User confused about EMI calculation, interest cost, why they're rejected
→
Short-Term Signal
Support tickets spike, app abandonment, negative reviews
→
Retention Impact
First→Second loan conversion drops to 20-30%. User never becomes repeat borrower.
→
Business Impact
CLV collapses. Acquisition spend wasted. Competitive defection to Bajaj/MoneyTap.

Problem Breakdown

CLV collapses after the first loan

  • Lever A broken: Retention → 0. Bad first-loan experience means user never returns in 3-5 day window. Switches to competitor.
  • Lever B broken: Frequency stays at 1. Habit loop never forms. No trust built. Acquisition spend doesn't compound.

Root causes of first-loan failure

  • Terms not explained upfront (interest rates shown, but users don't understand effective cost)
  • EMI calculation opaque — users surprised at monthly commitment
  • Eligibility criteria unclear — rejection feels arbitrary, no explanation given
  • Lengthy application with 60%+ abandonment rate
  • Support response time exceeds 24 hours, adding friction
03 — Stakeholders

Who is directly affected?

Stakeholder 1 → User / Borrower

Wants simple, transparent borrowing. A single failure (hidden fees, confusing process, unfair rejection) destroys trust that takes many good experiences to rebuild. Zero switching cost — Bajaj Finserv is one tap away.

↑ Better first-loan clarity → stays, reorders, forms habit

Stakeholder 2 → Kreditbee

Needs habitual borrowers for unit economics to work. Every churned first-loan user is acquisition cost with zero LTV return. High support tickets and confused users signal product friction killing growth.

↑ Higher clarity → lower churn, higher repeat rate, better CLV

04 — Solution

What product should build

Transparency and risk assessment tools already exist. The gap isn't financing capability — it's clarity and trust-building. Users need to understand the journey before they commit.

Solution 1 · Pre-Application Transparency Dashboard

A live eligibility preview before users submit — show them what they'd qualify for and why.

  1. User enters income and desired loan amount
  2. App shows: "You likely qualify for ₹50,000-₹100,000 based on your profile"
  3. Break down interest cost: "6-month loan: ₹X total interest vs 12-month: ₹Y"
  4. Show EMI upfront: "If you borrow ₹50k, your monthly payment is ₹XXX"
  5. Explain criteria met: "✓ Income verified ✓ Credit score 680+"

Solution 2 · Rejection Clarity + Improvement Roadmap

When users are rejected, surface exact reason and give them a path to qualify.

  1. Surface reason clearly: "Your credit score is 580. We require 650+ for this loan amount"
  2. Give actionable next steps: "Reduce outstanding debt by ₹X to improve eligibility"
  3. Show timeline: "Check back in 30-60 days. Your score should improve by then"
  4. Allow retry with lower amount: "You qualify for ₹10k loan now as a trust-builder"
  5. Track improvement: Show credit score trend and eligibility progression over time

Solution 3 · Post-Loan Engagement Loop

After first successful loan, build habit and trust for second borrowing.

  1. Track milestones: "You've paid 3 EMIs on time. Great progress!"
  2. Show eligibility growth: "Your credit improved to 670. You now qualify for ₹150k"
  3. Proactive outreach: "Need funds? You're pre-approved for ₹75k at 5.2% rate"
  4. Gamify trust: "Loan streak: 12 on-time payments. Unlocked: better rates tier"
05 — Success Metrics

How we know it's working

Support tickets per user is the fastest feedback signal. First→Second loan conversion within 6 months is the slower but truer measure of whether CLV is fixed at root.

Key Metrics to Track

  • First→Second Loan Conversion: % of users who take second loan within 6 months (target: 40-50%)
  • Application Abandonment Rate: Reduce from 60%+ to <30%
  • Support Tickets per User: Decrease from clarity confusion (target: -50%)
  • Rejection Reapplication Rate: % of rejected users who improve and reapply (target: 20%+)
  • CLV per New User: Total revenue per acquired user (target: +40%)
