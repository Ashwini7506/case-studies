Kreditbee CLV

How Product Design Unlocks Customer Lifetime Value

01 — Framework

What Actually Drives CLV?

Customer Lifetime Value = Loan Repayment Success Rate × Repeat Loans per User

Lever A — Our Focus
Avg. Loan Repayment Success Rate
Lever B — Our Focus
Avg. Repeat Loans per User

Lever A: Repayment Success

A user who never borrows a second loan has contributed minimally to CLV. Kreditbee's biggest leak is users who take a first loan, have a bad experience, and never return. Fixing first-loan experience is the fastest way to unlock repeat borrowing.

Lever B: Repeat Borrowing

Unit economics require repeat loans. A user who borrows once every 6 months will generate far more lifetime value than a user who borrows once and leaves. The problem: users don't trust Kreditbee enough to come back because they don't understand the platform.

02 — The Problem

Where CLV is Actually Breaking

40-50% Users who never apply for a second loan after initial confusion or rejection
3-5 days Critical window after first application before user loses interest
20-30% Users who stop using the app immediately after rejection
100K+ Monthly users who could borrow again but don't

Users don't trust Kreditbee because the loan experience is opaque. They don't understand interest rates, EMI calculations, eligibility criteria, or why they were rejected. After a bad first experience, switching costs nothing.

Loan application process lacks clarity and transparency

CLV collapses. Acquisition spend wasted. Competitive defection.

Problem Breakdown

Clarity broken — First loan experience is confusing
  • Interest rates shown, but users don't understand effective cost
  • EMI calculation opaque. Users surprised at commitment size.
  • Eligibility criteria not clear upfront. Rejection feels arbitrary.
Conversion broken — Users don't return
  • No reason given for rejection. User assumes disqualified forever.
  • No visibility into when eligibility might improve.
  • Trust not built. Easy to switch to competitors.

04 — Solution

What Product Should Build

The gap isn't financing capability. The gap is clarity and trust-building. Users need to understand the journey, terms, and their chances before they commit.

Solution 1: Pre-Application Transparency Dashboard

Before users apply, show them exactly what they'd qualify for and why.

  1. User enters income and loan amount needed
  2. App shows: "You likely qualify for ₹50,000-₹100,000. Here's why."
  3. Break down interest rate: "6-month loan: ₹X in interest."
  4. Show EMI upfront: "Your monthly payment is ₹XXX."
  5. Explain criteria: "You meet income, employment, and credit requirements."

Solution 2: Rejection Clarity and Next Steps

When users are rejected, give them a roadmap to qualify.

  1. Surface reason clearly: "Your credit score is 580. We require 650+."
  2. Give actionable steps: "Pay off ₹X in outstanding debt."
  3. Show timeline: "Check back in 30 days."
  4. Allow retry: Enable reapplication after improvements
  5. Build trust: Offer lower-amount loans as trust builders

Solution 3: Post-Loan Engagement Loop

After first successful loan, build habit and trust for second borrowing.

  1. Track repayment milestones: "You've paid 3 EMIs on time."
  2. Show eligibility growth: "You now qualify for ₹150k."
  3. Proactive outreach: "Need funds? You qualify for ₹75k more."
  4. Gamify trust: "Loan streak: 12 on-time payments."
05 — Success Metrics

How We Know It's Working

The fastest feedback loop is user confusion (support tickets, app abandonment). The truest measure is whether users return for a second loan.

→
App abandonment ↓
→
First loan completion ↑
Rejection clarity ↑
→

→
Second loan rate ↑

Key Metrics to Track

  • First→Second Loan Conversion: % of users who take a second loan within 6 months (target: 40-50%)
  • Support Tickets per User: Decrease from confusion (target: -40%)
  • Rejection Reapplication Rate: % of rejected users who reapply (target: 20%+)
  • CLV per New User: Total revenue per acquired user (target: +30%)
